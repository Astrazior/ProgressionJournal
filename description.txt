[h1]Progression Journal[/h1]

[b]A client-side progression helper for Terraria/tModLoader.[/b]

This mod adds an in-game journal with manually curated equipment recommendations for each class at every stage of progression.
It is designed as a quick reference during gameplay, so you do not have to open the wiki every few minutes.

[h2]Features[/h2]
[list]
[*]Equipment recommendations for melee, ranged, magic, and summoner at each progression stage
[*]Separate rating tiers: strong, usable, weak, and very weak
[*]Combat buffs page with stations, passives, potions, food, flasks, and permanent upgrades
[*]Item acquisition panel: drops, shops, recipes, crafting stations, and conditions
[*]Visual condition icons for events, moon phases, and other progression checks
[*]Fully usable in-game through a dedicated journal interface
[*]Custom build system with saving, import, and export support
[*]Build sharing through JSON format or directly through the in-game chat
[*]Support for items from other mods in custom builds
[*]Search and filtering tools for easier build setup
[*]Support for mods through separate profiles
[/list]

[h2]Build System[/h2]

Starting with version [b]2.0.0[/b], the mod includes a full build system. You can now create your own custom character builds, adjust them to your playstyle, and share them directly with other players.

[img]https://i.imgur.com/PV7pm66.gif[/img]

The build system allows you to create your own custom character builds: choose equipment, accessories, weapons, consumables, and other elements for a specific class or playstyle.

Builds can be shared in two ways:
[list]
[*]through JSON format for import and export;
[*]directly through the in-game chat.
[/list]

Builds are attached to the active progression profile, class, and stage. Profiles can define modded classes such as Rogue and can be copied and edited in-game.

[h2]Profile System[/h2]

Starting with version [b]2.1.0[/b], the mod now includes a profile system.

Due to numerous requests, I decided to add profiles for content mods. I will say this right away: this is not full support, and as I have said before, there will be no complete manual support from me. I consider it madness to try to properly evaluate the sheer number of items large mods add, and then continue maintaining all of that after updates.

That is why I made a system that approaches the problem differently.

Mod profiles are structured somewhat differently. Instead of the usual division by item effectiveness, they rely on two blocks:

[list]
[*][b]Recommendations[/b] — official recommendations from an official source. Usually this means the mod's wiki, provided it has ready-made class setups.
[*][b]New Equipment and Buffs[/b] — items and buffs that are determined by the mod itself.
[/list]

[img]https://i.imgur.com/n7OK3RO.gif[/img]

How does it work? The mod analyzes conditions, bosses, their loot, materials, recipes, and other progression connections. In an ideal case, it looks something like this:

[b]boss → new material → new armor or weapon[/b]

And in most cases, that is indeed roughly how it works. However, not all modded progression is that straightforward:

[b]boss → new enemy or new drop from an old enemy → new material → new armor or weapon[/b]

Such chains are already noticeably harder for the system to match correctly, which means the result may not always be accurate.

Mod profiles do not have ratings like “strong”, “usable”, “weak”, or “very weak”. The idea is that the player decides how to move forward by looking at what becomes available after each stage.

This feature is experimental. It will be updated, refined, improved, or changed over time. Do not expect it to work flawlessly. It is not a cure-all — at least not yet.

[h2]Important[/h2]
[list]
[*]This is a [b]client-side[/b] mod
[*]The server does not need to install it
[*]It does not change vanilla progression balance; it works as a reference tool
[*]Wiki and community profiles may be incomplete or become outdated after another mod updates
[*][b]At the moment, the system supports Calamity, Thorium, and Fargo's Souls profiles. This is not full manual support for every item and class. I cannot guarantee perfect accuracy of recommendations, as major content mods add a huge amount of equipment, mechanics, and non-standard progression conditions.[/b]
[/list]
